About

The story behind the lens

Why I photograph weddings

There's a moment in every wedding that no one notices. A glance between parents. A friend wiping tears before anyone sees. The groom's hands trembling as he waits. These are the moments I live for — the quiet, unscripted instances that reveal who people truly are when they think no one is watching.

I started One Time Photography with a simple belief: weddings are not performances. They're gatherings of love, anxiety, joy, and a thousand unspoken emotions. My job isn't to direct these moments — it's to recognize them and preserve them before they vanish into memory.

I don't believe in asking people to "look natural." Natural happens when you're not being watched. That's why I work quietly, moving through ceremonies and celebrations like a guest who happens to have a camera. No bright lights. No loud instructions. Just presence and patience.

The approach

Every wedding I photograph begins long before the first shot. I spend time understanding the families, the dynamics, the small details that matter to them. Some couples want their grandmother's hands documented. Others care about a childhood home in the background. These details shape how I see the day.

On the wedding day itself, I arrive early and stay late. I watch before I shoot. I notice the nervous energy of getting-ready rooms, the chaos of last-minute preparations, the first moment a parent sees their child as a bride or groom. These are the images that couples tell me they treasure most — not the posed portraits, but the real, raw, unrepeatable moments.

I photograph weddings across India and beyond — from intimate ceremonies in family courtyards to elaborate destination celebrations at palace hotels. The scale changes, but my approach remains the same: calm, focused, intentional.

The team

While I lead every wedding we document, I work with a carefully selected team of photographers and cinematographers who share my philosophy. We've worked together for years, developing an unspoken language that allows us to cover every angle without getting in each other's way — or yours.

Each team member is chosen not just for their technical skill, but for their temperament. Weddings are high-pressure environments, and the last thing you need is a photographer adding to the stress. We pride ourselves on being a calming presence, the vendors you don't have to worry about.

Beyond the camera

When I'm not photographing weddings, you'll find me traveling to places with no signal, reading poetry I don't fully understand, or attempting to cook dishes that require more patience than I possess. I believe that a rich life outside of work makes for better work — every experience, every observation, every moment of wonder feeds back into how I see the world through a lens.

Photography found me, not the other way around. And weddings? They chose me. There's something about being trusted with the most significant day of someone's life that never stops feeling like a privilege. It's a responsibility I take seriously, not with solemnity, but with genuine care.

A personal note

I often get asked what makes a great wedding photograph. My answer is always the same: it's not about perfect lighting or flawless composition (though those help). It's about truth. A great photograph tells you something real about the people in it. It makes you feel what they felt. Years from now, when you look at your wedding photos, I want you to be transported — not to remember a performance, but to relive a feeling.

If you're looking for a photographer who will blend into your celebration and emerge with images that feel like memories — not poses — I'd love to hear from you. Let's have a conversation about your wedding, your families, and the moments that matter most to you.
